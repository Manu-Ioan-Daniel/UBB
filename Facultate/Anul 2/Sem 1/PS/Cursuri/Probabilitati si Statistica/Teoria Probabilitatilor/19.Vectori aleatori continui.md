
### Definitie functie de densitate
 $f_{(X,Y)} : \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^+$ este **functia de densitate** a vectorului aleator continuu $(X, Y)$, daca
$$
F_{(X,Y)}(x, y) = P(X \le x, Y \le y) = \displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{x} \int_{-\infty}^{y} f_{(X,Y)}(s, t)dsdt \quad \forall x, y \in \mathbb{R},
$$
unde $F_{(X,Y)}$ este functia de repartitie a vectorului aleator $(X, Y)$.

### Proprietati ale Functiei de Densitate a unui Vector Aleator Continuu $(X, Y)$:

1. $f_{(X,Y)}(s, t) \ge 0 \quad \forall s, t \in \mathbb{R}$.
2. Norma:$\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{(X,Y)}(u, v)dudv = 1.$
3. $F_{(X,Y)}$ este functie continua pe $\mathbb{R}^2$.
4. Daca $F_{(X,Y)}$ este derivabila partial în $(x, y)$, atunci are loc:
$$
\displaystyle \frac{\partial^2 F_{(X,Y)}(x, y)}{\partial x \partial y} = f_{(X,Y)}(x, y).
$$
5. Probabilitatea ca $(X, Y)$ să aparțină unei regiuni $M$:
$$
P((X, Y) \in M) = \displaystyle \iint_{M} f_{(X,Y)}(u, v)dudv, \quad M \subset \mathbb{R}^2,
$$
pentru care integrala exista.

### Exemplul 1

Fie $f_{(X,Y)} : \mathbb{R}^2 \to [0, \infty)$ densitatea de probabilitate a vectorului aleator $(X, Y)$ definita prin
$$
f_{(X,Y)}(x, y) = \displaystyle
\begin{cases}
6xy, & \text{daca } 0 \le x \le 1, 0 \le y \le \sqrt{x} \\
0, & \text{altfel}.
\end{cases}
$$
Să se determine $P(X \le 0.5 \le Y)$.

$$
P(X\leq0.5\leq Y) = P(X\leq0.5,Y\geq0.5)=\int_{-\infty}^{0.5} \int_{0.5}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx =\int_{-\infty}^{0} \int_{0.5}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx \ +
$$
$$
+ \int_{0}^{0.5} \int_{0.5}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx = \int_{0}^{0.5} \int_{0.5}^{\sqrt{ x }} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx = \int_{0}^{0.25} \int_{0.5}^{\sqrt{ x }} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx  +\int_{0.25}^{0.5} \int_{0.5}^{\sqrt{ x }} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx  
$$
$$
=\int_{0.25}^{0.5} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{ x }} 6xy \, dy \, dx =3\int_{0.25}^{0.5} x^{3} \, dx = \frac{3}{4}x^{4}\Big |_{0.25}^{0.5} = \dots 
$$
#### Metoda 2
$$
0.5\leq y\leq \sqrt{ x } \implies \sqrt{ x }\geq0.5\implies x\geq0.25\implies(x,y) \in M ,\quad \text{unde } M=\{(x,y)|0.25\leq x\leq0.5,\ 0.5\leq y\leq \sqrt{ x }\}
$$
$$
P(X\leq0.5\leq Y)=P((X,Y) \in M) = \int_{0.25}^{0.5} \int_{0.5}^{\sqrt{ x }} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy  \, dx =\dots
$$
### Functii de densitate marginale
- $\displaystyle f_{X}(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dy,\forall \  x \in \mathbb{R}$
- $\displaystyle f_{Y}(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y) \, dx,\forall y \in \mathbb{R}$
#### Exemplu 2(distributia normala bidimensionala)
Distributia normala bidimensionala standard (X,Y) are functia de densitate:
$$
f_{(X,Y)}(x,y)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\exp\left(-\frac{x^{2}+y^{2}}{2}\right)
$$
$$
f_{X}(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2\pi}\exp\left( -\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)\exp\left( -\frac{y^{2}}{2} \right) \, dy = \frac{1}{2\pi}\exp\left( -\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\left( -\frac{y^{2}}{2} \right) \, dy  
$$
$$
= \frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2\pi }}\exp\left( -\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right) \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2\pi }}\exp\left( -\frac{y^{2}}{2} \right) \, dy = \frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2\pi }}\exp\left( -\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)  \implies X \sim N(0,1)
$$
$$
\text{similar } f_{Y}(y) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2\pi }}\exp\left( -\frac{y^{2}}{2} \right) \implies Y \sim N(0,1)
$$
### Independenta Variabilelor Aleatoare Continue

Variabilele aleatoare continue $X$ (cu functia de densitate $f_X$) si $Y$ (cu functia de densitate $f_Y$) sunt **independente** $\iff$ $f_{(X,Y)}(x, y) = \displaystyle f_X(x) f_Y(y) \quad \forall x, y \in \mathbb{R}$, unde $f_{(X,Y)}$ este functia de densitate a vectorului aleator $(X, Y)$.

#### Exemplu 3(distributia uniforma bidimensionala)
## Exemplul 3: Distributia Uniforma pe o Regiune Dreptunghiulara

Vectorul aleator $(X, Y)$ are **distributie uniforma** pe $I = [a_1, b_1] \times [a_2, b_2]$, cu $a_1, a_2, b_1, b_2 \in \mathbb{R}$, $a_1 < b_1$, $a_2 < b_2$, daca functia de densitate comuna este:

$$
f_{(X,Y)}(x, y) = \displaystyle
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{(b_1 - a_1)(b_2 - a_2)}, & \text{daca } (x, y) \in I \\
0, & \text{daca } (x, y) \notin I.
\end{cases}
$$
daca $x \in [a1,b1]:$
$$
f_X(x) = \displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{(X,Y)}(x, y) dy = \int_{a_2}^{b_2} \frac{1}{(b_1 - a_1)(b_2 - a_2)} dy
$$
$$
f_X(x) = \displaystyle \frac{1}{(b_1 - a_1)(b_2 - a_2)} [y]_{a_2}^{b_2} = \frac{b_2 - a_2}{(b_1 - a_1)(b_2 - a_2)} = \frac{1}{b_1 - a_1}.
$$
Daca $x \notin [a_1, b_1]$, atunci $f_{(X,Y)}(x, y) = 0$, deci integrala este 0.

$$
f_X(x) = \displaystyle
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{b_1 - a_1}, & \text{daca } x \in [a_1, b_1] \\
0, & \text{daca } x \in \mathbb{R} \setminus [a_1, b_1].
\end{cases}
$$
$$
f_Y(y) = \displaystyle
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{b_2 - a_2}, & \text{daca } y \in [a_2, b_2] \\
0, & \text{daca } y \in \mathbb{R} \setminus [a_2, b_2].
\end{cases}
$$

**Concluzie:**
$\implies X \sim \text{Unif}[a_1, b_1]$, $Y \sim \text{Unif}[a_2, b_2]$.
Deoarece $f_{(X,Y)}(x, y) = f_X(x) f_Y(y)$ , variabilele aleatoare $X$ si $Y$ sunt independente.
