## Descriptive Writing

- when you need to describe something/enlist key features
- list the most important points of something

Formed of:
- introduction
- description of process/system
- point/step 1/outcome
- point/step 2/outcome
- point/step 3/outcome
- conclusion

## Analytical writing

- when you need to explain something
- list the most important points which explain in my opinion explain the situation
- look at the facts, justify **how and why** not just **who/when/what**

Formed of:
- introduction
- definitions(any topic-specific terms that not everyone knows)
- most important point->example + evidence + reason 1  + example + evidence + reason 2
- next point->example + evidence + reason 1  + example + evidence + reason 2
- conclusion

## Comparison/evaluation

- when there s anything related to comparing stuff, this is it
- decide on 2 aspects and define them
- evaluate which aspect is better and provide justifiable arguments

Formed of:
- introduction
- state/define aspects
- aspect 1: Subj A vs Subj B
- aspect 2: Subj A vs Subj B
- conclusion

## Argument writing

- whenever there s a debate about something

Formed of:

- introduction - statement of issue
- statement giving opinion
- define terms
- point 1 explain + evidence
- point 2 explain + evidence
- conclusion
